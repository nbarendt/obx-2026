## 2. Arrival & departure: the Saturday traffic problem

> **You are arriving on a Saturday — the single worst day to approach the Outer Banks in July.** This section is the most important logistics page in this guide.

### Why Saturday is the problem

- **There is exactly one road in from the north**, and it funnels over the **Wright Memorial Bridge** (US-158). Everything from Virginia and points north — including you — converges on it.
- Nearly every OBX rental turns over on Saturday, so the entire island swaps occupants at once. The Outer Banks Visitors Bureau puts the crunch at **Saturdays, roughly 9 a.m.–6 p.m.**; the rental company pegs the worst of it at **12–4 p.m.**, sometimes lingering past 5.
- Backups approaching the bridge have reportedly **stretched close to 20 miles**. Reports of the bridge adding **2–5 hours** on a peak Saturday are common.
- **The true bottleneck isn't the bridge — it's the traffic light just past it**, at the **US-158 / NC-12 intersection in Kitty Hawk/Southern Shores**, where Duck- and Corolla-bound traffic turns left. Everything stacks up behind that light.

### ⚠️ No relief is coming

The **Mid-Currituck Bridge** — the proposed second crossing that would have taken pressure off the Wright Memorial Bridge — **is not happening for your trip and won't be for years.** A **June 2026 state audit** found $61M spent with no construction started; costs have ballooned from ~$491M to roughly **$1.2 billion**, and **NCDOT says no construction will occur until at least mid-2028.** Plan as though the Wright Memorial Bridge is the only northern option — because it is.

### 🔑 The move: take the southern route and skip the bridge entirely

**This is the single most valuable thing in this guide.** Because **Kill Devil Hills is *south* of the Kitty Hawk bottleneck**, you can approach from the south and bypass both the Wright Memorial Bridge *and* that traffic light.

The **Outer Banks Visitors Bureau explicitly recommends this** — their official guidance says that for visitors staying **south of Southern Shores (Kitty Hawk, Kill Devil Hills, Nags Head, Manteo, and south)**, an alternate route **"can save several hours,"** and that even though it's more miles, *you'll be moving instead of parked.*

**The route, in broad strokes:** instead of following VA-168 / US-158 southeast toward the Wright Memorial Bridge, stay inland and come at the Outer Banks from the west on **US-64 East**:

> **US-17 South** (through Suffolk, VA → **Edenton, NC**) → **US-64 East** (through Williamston / Plymouth / Columbia) → across the **Virginia Dare Memorial Bridge** (5.2 miles over Croatan Sound — the longest bridge in NC) → **Roanoke Island / Manteo** → **Washington Baum Bridge** → you land in **Nags Head**, then head **north on US-158** a few miles to Kill Devil Hills.

*Confidence note: the route geometry above is my reconstruction from the Visitors Bureau, NCDOT, and multiple rental-company sources — verify the exact turns in Google Maps before you drive it. The **principle** (come in via US-64 through Manteo rather than over the Wright Memorial Bridge) is what's well-sourced and widely recommended.*

### 🧭 Making Google actually route you this way

**Why "Manteo" as a waypoint doesn't work:** Manteo is reachable *over* the Wright Memorial Bridge (down US-158 past your house, then west on US-64). So Google happily takes the bridge, drives past your door, and doubles back — the waypoint changes nothing. **You need a waypoint west of the Croatan Sound**, so the bridge route becomes an absurd backtrack.

**✅ Use this waypoint instead: `Columbia, NC 27925`**
- It sits **on US-64**, about 45 miles west of Manteo, west of the Alligator River.
- Reaching Columbia via the Wright Memorial Bridge would mean crossing the bridge and driving ~60 miles back west — Google won't do it. **This forces the inland approach.**
- It's directly *on* the natural line, so it costs you **no detour** once you've committed to the route.

**Alternates if you want to force it even earlier:** `Williamston, NC` (US-64/US-17 junction) or `Plymouth, NC`. Both are further west and even more forcing, but push you inland sooner.

**Drop the Manteo waypoint** and use Columbia alone. Google will then pick its own best inland path to Columbia — most likely either **I-95 → Rocky Mount → US-64 E**, or **US-17 S through Elizabeth City/Edenton → US-64 E**. Either is fine; both skip the bridge.

### ⚠️ The catch on this route: the Alligator River swing bridge

Between Columbia and Manns Harbor, US-64 crosses the **Lindsay C. Warren (Alligator River) Bridge** — and this is a real, current consideration:

- It is a **1960 swing-span drawbridge** that **opens for boat traffic** (~4,000 boats/year), causing **unpredictable delays** of minutes at a time.
- It's rated **structurally deficient** (not unsafe — just old), and has a **history of mechanical failures/jams**. When it jams, the detour is reportedly **~100 miles via Elizabeth City.** Low probability, high pain.
- **A replacement is under construction right now** (began Feb 2025, Skanska, $450M) — but the new fixed-span high-rise **doesn't open to traffic until ~fall 2029.** You get the old bridge.
- **Good news:** NCDOT confirms that during construction, **the current bridge stays open to traffic.** The construction itself shouldn't block you.
- The stretch from **Columbia to Manns Harbor is two-lane, 55 mph, and actively patrolled.** Don't plan on making up time there.

**Net:** the US-64 route trades a *near-certain* multi-hour Saturday jam at the Wright Memorial Bridge for a *small* risk of a swing-bridge delay. That's usually a good trade — but **check [DriveNC.gov](https://drivenc.gov/) before you commit**, and it's the reason to keep a Plan B.

### 🛑 Richmond is your decision point

If you come down I-95, the fork is simple:
- **Continue I-95 South** (toward Rocky Mount / the inland US-64 route), **or**
- **Peel off onto I-64 East** toward Norfolk and the Wright Memorial Bridge.

That fork is roughly **2–2.5 hours out**. **Check live traffic there and decide** — this is your last cheap opportunity to switch.

**Bonus:** the US-64 route drops you in through **Manteo and right past Wanchese** — the aquarium, Festival Park, and the fish houses. If you want to stock the fridge on the way in, you're driving straight past them.

## 3. Getting there from Cleveland: routes, waypoints & the Friday stopover

*Driving from **Fairview Park, OH** (Cleveland) to **Kill Devil Hills (Milepost 8)**, arriving on a **Saturday**, with a **Friday overnight**. Distances/times from Google Maps, summer 2026 — re-verify before departure.*

### The core problem, in one line

The default route sends you **through Washington DC** and then **over the Wright Memorial Bridge** — the two worst things you can do on a summer Saturday. Both are fixable, and fixing them costs almost nothing.

### 🔑 The waypoint that does the real work: `Columbia, NC 27925`

**Manteo does NOT work as a waypoint.** Manteo is reachable *over* the Wright Memorial Bridge (down US-158, past your house, then back west on US-64) — so Google happily takes the bridge and doubles back. The waypoint is satisfied; nothing changes.

**`Columbia, NC` works** because it sits **on US-64, west of the Alligator River**. Reaching it via the Wright Memorial Bridge would mean crossing and driving ~60 miles back west — Google won't do it. This forces the inland approach and **keeps you off the bridge entirely.**

> **Waypoints:** `Staunton, VA` **+** `Columbia, NC`

*(Don't be thrown when the signs say "**US-158 WEST**" at Whalebone Junction — that's the signed direction, but you're physically driving **north** up the beach toward MP 8. That's correct.)*

### 🌉 Crossing Hampton Roads: which one?

Once you're on the Peninsula, you **must** cross the water. Three highway options:

1. **⭐ Monitor-Merrimac Bridge-Tunnel (I-664)** — **what Google routes you on, and the right call.** VDOT's own current guidance tells Outer Banks traffic to use **I-664/MMMBT as the alternative to the HRBT to save time during construction.** It carries about **half** the HRBT's daily traffic and is toll-free.
2. **Hampton Roads Bridge-Tunnel (I-64)** — **actively avoid.** The notorious one, and it's mid-way through a **massive expansion project running into 2027**. That's exactly why VDOT is diverting OBX traffic away from it.
3. **James River Bridge (US-17/258/32)** — **your backup.** The **only tunnel-free crossing**, free, and VDOT calls it a smart alternative when both tunnels are congested. From I-64 take **exit 263 (Mercury Blvd / Route 258)** onto US-17 S — and you're headed down US-17 anyway, so it's barely a detour.
   - ✅ **Confirmed clear for your trip.** VDOT's wire-rope replacement project required two 100-hour full closures — both were completed **in January and February 2024**, ahead of schedule. The project is finished and **no further full closures are scheduled.**
   - ⚠️ Only caveat: it's a **lift bridge** that **opens on vessel demand with no restricted hours** — so it can go up at any time. Minutes, not hours.

**On the road:** I-64 has electronic signs before the split showing live travel times via **HRBT vs. MMMBT**. Glance at them; if the MMMBT looks ugly, exit 263 to the James River Bridge is your out.

### ⚠️ The one tail risk on this route: the Alligator River swing bridge

Between Columbia and Manns Harbor, US-64 crosses the **Lindsay C. Warren (Alligator River) Bridge** — a **1960 swing-span drawbridge** that opens for boat traffic (~4,000 boats/year) and has a **history of mechanical jams**; when it jams, the detour is reportedly **~100 miles via Elizabeth City.** Its fixed-span replacement is under construction but **doesn't open until ~fall 2029.** NCDOT confirms **the current bridge stays open** during construction. Low probability, high pain — **check [DriveNC.gov](https://drivenc.gov/) that morning.**

*Also note: US-64 from **Columbia to Manns Harbor is two-lane, 55 mph, and actively patrolled.** Don't plan on making up time there. The **NC-37 → NC-94 → Scuppernong Rd** stretch (~35 mi) is also rural two-lane with no passing.*

*Options **on the chosen route only** — the I-70 → US-522 → I-81 → I-64 → US-17 → US-64 corridor (see *Getting there from Cleveland*). Nothing here is more than a few minutes off the highway unless flagged. **This is a menu, not a schedule** — stop where the crew needs to. ### Friday (the long day): I-70 into the Shenandoah Valley

**Hancock, MD — right at the I-70 → US-522 turn.** The **[Western Maryland Rail Trail](https://www.canaltrust.org/discoveryarea/western-maryland-rail-trail/)** and the C&O Canal towpath meet the **Little Tonoloway** day-use area here: restrooms, picnic tables, and a flat paved path to walk out the stiffness. Clean and quick, at roughly the one-third mark of the drive. *(Longer break: **Berkeley Springs, WV** and its state-park warm springs are ~10–15 min south on US-522.)*

**Winchester, VA (I-81, ~exit 313) — best lunch on the north half.** The **[Old Town Winchester Walking Mall](https://visitwinchesterva.com/locations/old-town-winchester/)** (Loudoun St pedestrian mall) is two traffic-free blocks of cafés and restaurants with four parking garages — easy to feed a big group and let kids move. Casual to sit-down, something for every eater.

**New Market / Mount Jackson cluster (I-81, ~exits 264–273) — the fun-stop zone.** Three good options within a few miles:

- **[Route 11 Potato Chips](https://www.rt11.com/viewing)** (Mount Jackson, exit 273) — free **fry-viewing** windows in the shop and endless samples; a genuinely fun ~15-minute kid stop. Mon–Sat 9–5, **closed Sundays. Call ahead — 540-477-9664 — to make sure they're cooking that day** (they don't fry every day); groups of 15+ should call regardless.
- **[Shenandoah Caverns](https://shenandoahcaverns.com/visitor-info/)** (exit 269) — a 1-hour, one-mile guided cave tour at a steady 56°F, and **the only Virginia cavern with elevator access** (no stairs — good for grandparents and strollers). Summer 9–6 (last tour ~5). A proper way to break the day.
- **[New Market Battlefield / VA Museum of the Civil War](https://www.vmi.edu/museums-and-archives/virginia-museum-of-the-civil-war/)** (exit 264) — Civil War museum plus walkable fields on a bluff over the valley. Open daily 9–5; **540-740-3101** (confirm — guided-tour availability varies).
- *Bigger detour:* **[Luray Caverns](https://luraycaverns.com/plan/)** is the famous one, but it's ~25–30 min **east** off I-81 (via US-211) and pricier (~$36 adult, ~$18 kids 6–12, under 6 free). Worth it only if caverns are the point of the day, not a quick stop.

**Staunton, VA (I-81, ~exit 222) — your waypoint, and a good late-day stretch.** You turn onto **I-64 East** here anyway. Walkable downtown with restaurants, plus the **[Frontier Culture Museum](https://frontiermuseum.org/)** — a large open-air living-history site with free on-property shuttles (9–5; ~$12 adult, $7 kids 6–12, under 6 free; **540-332-7850**). *(Harrisonburg, ~exit 245, also has an easy walkable downtown if lunch lands earlier.)*

**Charlottesville, VA (I-64, ~35 min east of Staunton).** If lunch falls on the I-64 leg, the pedestrian **Downtown Mall** sits close to the interstate with plenty of food. (Monticello is here too, but it's a half-day, not a travel-day stop.)

### Saturday (the short leg): US-17 through northeastern NC

You'll cross the Monitor-Merrimac early and roll down US-17 South with **hours to spare before the 4–6 PM check-in** (see *Arrival & departure*). Two waterfront towns sit right on your road — better places to burn that time than idling near the house:

- **Elizabeth City, NC (US-17) — [Museum of the Albemarle](https://www.museumofthealbemarle.com/plan-visit).** Free, Mon–Sat 10–4 (**closed Sundays and July 4**), on the Pasquotank riverfront across from Waterfront Park. A quick, free, air-conditioned leg-stretch with restrooms.
- **Edenton, NC (US-17) — the pretty one.** A historic town on Albemarle Sound: **Colonial Waterfront Park** (playground + fishing pier for the kids), a walkable downtown of ~40 shops and restaurants, and the **[Edenton State Historic Site visitor center](https://visitedenton.com/explore-edenton/historic-sites-attractions/)** (Tue–Sat 9–5). The best lunch-and-stretch on the NC leg if you've got the time.

**One Saturday caveat:** it's a peak beach-turnover day and the **Alligator River swing bridge** is still downstream of you (see *Arrival & departure*). A relaxed stop in Edenton is fine — but glance at **[DriveNC.gov](https://drivenc.gov/)** before you linger, so a bridge opening or jam doesn't turn a lunch break into a problem.

## 6. Weather & packing

**Typical late July in Kill Devil Hills** (climate averages — *not* a forecast for your exact dates):
- Highs generally **low-80s to low-90s °F**, lows **low-to-mid 70s**. Average high ~86°F.
- **Humid**, with heat index occasionally ~100–105°F midday.
- **Near-daily chance of afternoon/evening thunderstorms** — usually fast-moving; mornings are the calmest, sunniest window.
- **Ocean ~78–82°F** — warm for swimming. **High UV** — sun protection is essential.

**Check the real forecast** ~3–5 days out (it isn't reliable further ahead): **[NWS point forecast for KDH](https://forecast.weather.gov/MapClick.php?lat=36.0032&lon=-75.6655)**. For tropical activity, watch the **[National Hurricane Center](https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/)** — July is early season, but a distant storm can still raise surf and rip currents.

**Packing highlights:** reef-safe sunscreen (lots), wide-brim hats, UV shirts for toddlers, after-sun; a **beach wagon** (soft sand + gear + little kids); **pop-up shade/umbrellas** (down by ~7 p.m., can't block lifeguard sightlines — see *Your beach*); Coast Guard–approved **puddle-jumpers**; **water shoes** for the sound; bug spray (soundside/woods); baby monitor/white-noise for the big house; deck games; a small crabbing kit (see *Fishing & crabbing*).

**Rip currents (read with the group):** common here. **Check the stand's flag:** *yellow = caution, red = no swimming.* If caught: **don't fight it, don't panic** — it pulls you out, not under. **KDH beach rules (enforced):**
- **No glass** (cans/plastic fine; alcohol allowed).
- **Remove all gear daily by ~7 p.m.** (tents/chairs/umbrellas) or it may be discarded.

- **No bonfires** without a permit ([Fire Dept.](https://www.kdhnc.com/) 252-480-4060); **fireworks that fly/explode are illegal**; **no camping.**
- **Beach driving is off-season only (Oct 1–Apr 30)** — *no 4×4 on the sand in July.*

**Sea turtles:** July is nesting/hatching season. Report nests/crawls/strandings to **[N.E.S.T.](https://www.nestonline.org/)**; fill holes and keep beach-facing lights low at night. Kids love being "turtle guardians."

## 10. Stargazing & the night sky

*The Outer Banks gets genuinely dark once you're away from the Beach Road lights, and summer is Milky-Way season. The astronomy below is public 2026 fact — map the dates to your own nights, skies permitting (check the forecast under *Weather & packing*).*

**Where to look:** your own beach works if you face **east over the ocean**, away from town glow. For darker skies, drive ~15–20 min **south into Cape Hatteras National Seashore** — the **[Coquina Beach / Bodie Island](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Coquina+Beach+Bodie+Island+NC)** area has almost no lighting, and it only gets darker farther down Hatteras. Give your eyes ~20 minutes to adjust and use a **red flashlight** (it preserves night vision — and keep beach-facing lights low for nesting sea turtles; see *Your beach*).

**The Moon sets the schedule (July 2026):** New Moon **July 14**, First Quarter **July 21**, Full "Buck" Moon **July 29**. **Darkest, best skies are the nights around the new moon (mid-July) and again as the full moon wanes into early August.** Near July 29 the moonlight washes out faint objects — a great night to look *at* the Moon (binoculars show the craters), not for the Milky Way.

**Planets:**
- **Venus** — brilliant, low in the **west just after sunset.** The can't-miss target for kids.
- **Saturn** — rises late evening and stands **high in the south before dawn** by late July (its best showing of the month); a small scope shows the rings.
- **Mars** — reddish and **low in the east before dawn.**
- *(Jupiter sinks into the sunset glow and is gone by late July.)*

**Constellations & the Milky Way (mid-northern summer sky):**
- The **Summer Triangle** rides high overhead after dark — three bright stars: **Vega** (in Lyra), **Deneb** (in Cygnus, the Swan / "Northern Cross"), and **Altair** (in Aquila).
- Low in the **south**, red **Antares** anchors **Scorpius**; just to its left, the **"Teapot" of Sagittarius** points straight at the **bright core of the Milky Way** — from a dark beach you can trace the hazy band arching up from the southern horizon around 10–11 p.m.
- In the north-west evening, the **Big Dipper** points down to **Polaris**, the North Star; high in the west, follow the Dipper's handle to orange **Arcturus** ("arc to Arcturus").

**Meteor showers (temper expectations for 2026):** the **Southern Delta Aquariids** (peak ~**July 30**) and **Alpha Capricornids** (peak ~**July 30–31**, known for the odd bright fireball) are both active in late July — but their 2026 peak falls right after the **July 29 full moon**, so moonlight will drown out all but the brightest. Look **pre-dawn toward the south**, and you'll do better on the **darker nights earlier in the window.** (The famous Perseids don't peak until **Aug 12**, after most July trips.)

**Kid-friendly:** a free phone app (Stellarium, SkyView) that labels stars as you point it turns "which one is that?" into the whole activity — grandparents included.

## 15. Fishing & crabbing (pier, surf, and family sound crabbing)

![An Atlantic blue crab, the main catch for family crabbing on the sound](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Special:FilePath/Granchio%20blu%20%28Callinectes%20sapidus%29.jpg?width=900)

*Atlantic blue crab — the target for family crabbing. Photo: [Wikimedia Commons](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Granchio_blu_(Callinectes_sapidus).jpg).*

### Pier fishing (easiest with kids — no personal license needed)
Ocean piers carry a **"blanket" license**, so **no individual license is required** — pay the **daily fee**, and many rent rods and sell bait.
- **[Avalon Fishing Pier](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Avalon+Fishing+Pier+Kill+Devil+Hills)** — **MP 6, KDH** (~700 ft). **Closest to the house (~2 mi north)** — the natural default for a casual family morning.
- **[Nags Head Fishing Pier](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Nags+Head+Fishing+Pier)** — MP 11.5.
- **[Jennette's Pier](http://www.jennettespier.net/)** — MP 16.5 (rod rentals + bait). *Check plank-work status.*

Typical summer catches: spot, croaker, sea mullet, bluefish, flounder, Spanish mackerel, pompano.

### Surf fishing (right off your beach)
- **License:** anyone **16+** surf/sound fishing needs the **NC Coastal Recreational Fishing License (CRFL)** — buy online at the **[NC Wildlife Resources Commission](https://www.ncwildlife.gov/)** (search "Coastal Recreational Fishing License") or at a tackle shop.
- Fish **moving tides** and look for **sloughs/cuts** (darker troughs between sandbars). Early morning and evening are best.

### Sound fishing + family crabbing (calm, shallow, toddler-friendly)
The **sound is warmer, calmer, and shallower** than the ocean — ideal for little kids and crabbing. **No lifeguards on the sound**, little shade — bring shade, water, sunscreen, and water shoes.

**Best nearby crabbing/sound spots:**
- **[Colington canals](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Colington+Kill+Devil+Hills+NC)** (KDH, west of US-158) — a maze of saltwater canals locals rate among the best crabbing around. Residential — use small public access/parking and respect homeowners. ~10 min from the house.
- **["Little Bridge" (Melvin R. Daniels Bridge)](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Little+Bridge+Nags+Head+Manteo+Causeway)** on the Nags Head–Manteo Causeway (US-64) — a classic, very popular family crabbing platform.
- **Western Nags Head soundside** — pull-offs along US-158 between Jockey's Ridge and Jennette's; shallow grassy flats.
- **[KDH soundside accesses](https://www.kdhnc.com/1003/Soundside-Accesses)** — West Hayman St, West 3rd St, West Arch St, Dock St, and the **Moor Shores canoe/kayak launch** (good for launching a kayak/SUP too).
- **NC Aquarium Soundside Pier** (Manteo) — guided **"What's the Catch?"** fishing/crabbing programs; combine with your aquarium visit.
- **[Old Swimming Hole](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Old+Swimming+Hole+Manteo+Airport+Road)** (Airport Rd near the aquarium) — calm soundside spot great for toddlers wading.

**How to crab with kids (no boat, minimal gear):**
- **Chicken-necking:** tie a raw chicken neck/leg (or fish) to a string, lower it, wait, then **slowly** raise the crab and scoop with a long-handled net. Toddlers-to-grandparents can all join.
- **License note:** you **don't** need a license for hand-line/chicken-necking or "legging," and you don't need one to set **one crab pot per person from a private pier/shoreline** with owner permission. Follow **recreational size/harvest limits**; no selling your catch.
- Best on **moving water**, early morning or late afternoon. Keep a cooler with ice.

**Guided options (if you'd rather someone else rigs it):**
- **[Outer Banks Fishing Outfitters](https://obxfishingoutfitters.com/)** — inshore/nearshore charters. **Guest discount available** — call **252-599-1739** and mention your rental company.
- **OBX Tiki Time** (Manteo Marina) — runs **guided crabbing trips** as well as dolphin/sunset cruises.

**Bait, tackle, licenses, rod rentals:**
- **[TW's Bait & Tackle](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=TW's+Bait+and+Tackle+Nags+Head)** (Kitty Hawk **and** Nags Head) — licenses, bait (chicken necks, bloodworms, shrimp, mullet), crab nets/lines/traps, rod-and-reel combos.

## 18. Seafood cookout night — buying direct from the docks

If you want seafood that was **swimming that morning**, skip the grocery counter and go to a **fish house** — the place where commercial boats unload and the catch is cleaned and packed. On the OBX, that means **Wanchese**, the working fishing village at the south end of Roanoke Island. It doesn't court tourists; it's boatbuilders, fish houses, and packing docks. Buying there is about as close to off-the-boat as a visitor can get.

### 🥇 The dock-direct tier — Wanchese (~35–40 min from the house)

**[O'Neal's Sea Harvest](https://onealsseaharvest.com/)** — 618/622 Harbor Rd, Wanchese · **(252) 473-4535** · [📍 Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=O'Neal's+Sea+Harvest+Wanchese+NC)
The biggest fish house in Wanchese, directly on the harbor in the Seafood Industrial Park — a family-run wholesaler that hand-picks from the catch crossing its own docks each day and sells it to the public. It's an **open-air market where you can watch the crew cleaning and processing**; you can pick a whole fish and they'll clean it for you on the spot. They'll sell **steamed crabs by the bushel and shrimp by the pound**, and — great for your drive home — **they'll ice down your cooler at no charge**. There's also an on-site lunch counter (chalkboard specials that change with the catch).
- **Hours:** market generally **Mon–Sat, opens 10 a.m.** (closing is seasonal, ~3–4 p.m.); **closed Sunday.** Lunch ~11 a.m.–3:30 p.m. **Call for specials, prices, and availability** — and to confirm hours, which shift seasonally.
- **What reviewers say:** consistent raves about freshness ("straight off the boats"), unusual species you won't find elsewhere (sheepshead, triggerfish), fair prices, and helpful counter staff. Common gripes: it's **off the beaten path** (that's a deliberate trip, not a drive-by), it can be **packed** in season, and it's a working fish house — *it smells like one inside*, though there's outdoor seating.

**[Fresh Catch Seafood](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Fresh+Catch+Seafood+Mill+Landing+Road+Wanchese)** — 4331 Mill Landing Rd, Wanchese · **(252) 475-9888**
**Owned and operated by local commercial fishermen**, selling straight off their own fleet — arguably the purest "direct from the fisherman" option on the beach. Selection depends entirely on what came in: tuna, mahi, swordfish, croaker, bluefish, sea mullet, shrimp, oysters, clams, crabs, scallops. They also **vacuum-seal and pack** (including charter catches), which is handy if you want to take some home to Ohio.

*Geography note:* Wanchese is on the far side of Roanoke Island, past Manteo — so it's on the same run as the NC Aquarium, Festival Park, and the Manteo waterfront. **Landing Grill & Seafood Steam Bar** at Wanchese Marina (4457 Mill Landing Rd, **252-473-3247**) is right on the harbor if you want lunch on the water while you're down there.

### 🏠 Closest to the house

**[Billy's Seafood](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Billy's+Seafood+1341+Colington+Road+Kill+Devil+Hills)** — 1341 Colington Rd, Kill Devil Hills · **(252) 441-5978** · **~10 minutes away**
A KDH institution since 1971, just across the first bridge on Colington Road — **the same road as the Colington crabbing canals**, so combine the two. Fresh off-the-boat crabs, clams, shrimp, oysters, flounder, and soft shells depending on the day. **Local blue crabs are cooked daily and they're all #1 male crabs — call ahead to order crabs so they're ready when you arrive.** They also stock everything else you need for the cookout: seasonings, sauces, **beer & wine**, charcoal and grilling supplies, plus fishing gear.
- **Hours:** **9 a.m.–6 p.m. daily**, open April–November *(as of their latest posted hours — worth a confirming call).*
- **What reviewers say:** repeatedly called the best value on the beach — several note **shrimp at roughly half** what they paid elsewhere on the OBX. The **homemade tuna salad** has a cult following ("buy more than you think you'll need").
- **This is my pick for your group** — closest, open Sundays, cooks crabs to order, and you can buy the beer and charcoal in the same stop.

### 🐟 Nags Head / Kitty Hawk (good, and easier than Wanchese)

- **[Whalebone Seafood Market](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Whalebone+Seafood+Market+Grey+Eagle+Street+Nags+Head)** — 101 Grey Eagle St, Nags Head · **(252) 441-8808**. Family-run since 1985 out of the Daniels family's fishing fleet; **Wanchese fishermen bring their catch to Whalebone's own docks**, where it's hand-picked for the market. Their **to-go steamer dinners feed 1–10 people** (pick a combo or build your own), and they sell house-made cocktail sauce, compound butters, charcoal, and corn — nearly a turnkey cookout.
- **[Austin Fish Company (Austin Seafood)](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Austin+Fish+Company+Nags+Head)** — 3711 S Croatan Hwy, MP 12.5, Nags Head · **(252) 441-7412**. Longtime award-winner across from Jockey's Ridge (look for the **great white shark over the entrance** — the kids will spot it). Big variety: tuna, mahi, flounder, snapper, swordfish, grouper, crabmeat, shrimp, oysters, live lobsters. **Steamed and ready-to-cook options** too.
- **[Greentails Seafood Market & Kitchen](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Greentails+Seafood+Nags+Head)** — 3022 S Croatan Hwy (Pirate's Quay, MP 11.5), Nags Head · **(252) 715-6300**. Sources directly from Wanchese fishermen and fish houses; daily-changing case. Also a kitchen doing poke and lunch specials.
- **[Carawan Seafood Co.](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Carawan+Seafood+Kitty+Hawk)** — Shoreside Center, Kitty Hawk. Family-run since 1980, cleans and packs in-house (the giant **SHRIMP** sign is the landmark). Frequently named among the best markets in NC; **they'll clean a whole fish for you.**

### 🗓️ What's actually landing in late July

Ask what came in *that morning* — but broadly, mid-to-late summer means:
- **Likely:** **shrimp** (brown shrimp are a summer catch), **blue crab** (peak summer), **mahi-mahi**, **yellowfin tuna** (available year-round here), **Spanish mackerel**, **bluefish**, **black sea bass**, **clams**, **sea mullet/croaker**.
- **Maybe:** **flounder** (more of a late-summer fish — could be just coming on).
- **Probably not:** **oysters** (really an Oct–March thing), **green-tail shrimp** (a fall catch), and **soft-shell crabs** — soft-shell season is short and mostly *spring*, so don't plan the menu around them. If a market has them in July, treat it as a bonus.

*Confidence note: this is the general seasonal pattern from NC seafood guides, not a guarantee — availability swings with weather, regulations, and what the boats found. The one reliable method is to call the morning of and ask what's fresh.*

### 🍤 Buying for a crowd — practical tips

- **Call ahead, ideally the day before.** Bushels of crabs, large steamer pots, and big shrimp orders should be **pre-ordered** — especially at Billy's (crabs) and Whalebone (steamer dinners). Same-day walk-in for a 15+ person haul is how you end up with whatever's left.
- **Bring a big cooler.** O'Neal's will ice it down free; a cooler in the car is the difference between a 40-minute drive being fine and being a problem.
- **Go in the morning.** The catch is freshest, the selection is widest, and you avoid both the heat and the afternoon storms.
- **Ask them to clean/fillet it.** Most of these markets will do it while you wait — a huge time-saver when you're cooking for a crowd.
- **Rough quantities (rules of thumb, not gospel):** for a shrimp boil, plan roughly **½–¾ lb of head-on shrimp per adult** (less for little kids); a **bushel of blue crabs runs about 5–7 dozen**, which feeds a big table of dedicated pickers. When in doubt, tell the counter staff how many people you're feeding — they do this all day and will steer you right.
- **Crabs are a project, and that's the point.** Steamed blue crabs + newspaper on the deck table + mallets is a genuinely great multigenerational evening — toddlers can watch, teens compete, grandparents supervise. Have **melted butter, Old Bay, vinegar, paper towels, and a trash bowl** ready.
- **Don't forget the sides.** Corn, red potatoes, andouille sausage, lemons, cocktail sauce. Billy's and Whalebone can cover most of this in one stop.
- **Pair it with your own catch.** If the crabbing at Colington or Little Bridge goes well, supplement with market crabs so nobody goes hungry.

**Two-stop logic:** O'Neal's has the wow factor and the widest off-the-boat selection but is closed Sundays and ~40 minutes out; Billy's is 10 minutes away, open daily, and sells the beer, charcoal, and corn too. Plenty of groups use O'Neal's for the seafood and Billy's to fill the gaps.

## 20. Bikes — yes, bring them

The rack is worth it. KDH and Nags Head have an **extensive, flat, paved multi-use path network** ideal for mixed abilities:
- **Nags Head** has an ~**11-mile paved beach-road path** (quieter/scenic) plus a bypass path, connectable at stoplights.
- **KDH** paths run along the Beach Road and connect toward the **Wright Brothers Memorial loop** (flat, easy, via First Flight Airfield).
- **Wind tip:** ride **out into the wind** so the tired return leg is wind-at-your-back (summer breeze is usually from the south).
- Prefer not to haul them? **[Outer Banks Bicycle](https://www.outerbanksbicycle.com/)** and **[Ocean Atlantic Rentals](https://www.oceanatlanticrentals.com/)** deliver bikes (kids' bikes, trailers, tandems) to the house — and there's a **guest discount** (see *Rental company*). That materially changes the math on hauling a 4-bike rack down.
